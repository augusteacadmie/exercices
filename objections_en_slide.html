Unit 4 · Effective Argumentation · English

Handling objections
without losing the sale

EN

An objection is not a refusal — it's a request for more information. Click each one.

💸

Objection 1

"That's quite expensive."

▾

✕ What not to say

"Yes, but it's worth it, it's really good."

✓ Professional response

"This whisky is aged 18 years in oak — that maturation time is what you taste. The depth and smoothness you get here simply isn't there in a 12-year."

Key move : Never defend the price directly. Demonstrate what justifies it. The price becomes information, not an obstacle.

🤷

Objection 2

"I've never heard of this."

▸

✕ What not to say

"It's a great product, very popular right now."

✓ Professional response

"If you enjoy classic gins, this one will surprise you — it's much more floral and less bitter. Think of it as a step in a new direction."

Key move : Build a bridge from the known to the unknown. The unfamiliar becomes a discovery, not a risk.

🔄

Objection 3

"I usually have something else."

▸

✕ What not to say

"Oh, but this one is better, you should try it."

✓ Professional response

"Of course — and if you'd like something in the same style but with a little more depth, I could suggest… entirely up to you."

Key move : Never dismiss the guest's usual choice. Respect the habit, offer a bridge — and always return the decision to them.

Rule : Every objection has a structure — acknowledge, reframe, offer. Never argue. Never push. The guest always keeps the final word.
