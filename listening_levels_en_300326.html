Unit 1 · Taking Orders · English

Listening before hearing —
what the guest is really saying

EN

Every order has two levels. Click each signal to decode it.

💬

Signal 1 · Explicit

"Something refreshing."

▾

What they said

No product named. No category given. Just a sensation.

What they mean

They're giving you full latitude to recommend. This is your moment — take it with confidence.

Your move : "In that case, I'd suggest our Cucumber Cooler — gin, tonic and fresh mint. Very clean and light. Does that work?"

🔁

Signal 2 · Habit

"Same as last time."

▸

What they said

A shortcut. They enjoyed the last drink and don't want to think again.

What they mean

They trust you. They want to be recognised as much as they want the drink.

Your move : "The Negroni again — with the Campari well measured, just as before?" Naming the drink confirms you remember. That detail builds loyalty.

🤫

Signal 3 · Silence

The guest says nothing.

▸

What they said

Nothing. A pause. Eyes drifting — to the menu, the room, other guests' glasses.

What they mean

They are waiting to be guided. Silence is availability — not disinterest.

Your move : "Can I suggest something for you? Our house cocktail tonight is very popular — gin, yuzu and basil. Very light." You stepped in. That's the service.

⚡

Signal 4 · Mismatch

"A gin tonic... I think."

▸

What they said

A product name — but with hesitation. The words and the body language don't match.

What they mean

They named what they knew — not necessarily what they want. The hesitation is the real signal.

Your move : "Of course — are you after something classic and clean, or would you like something a little more original?" One question. Opens the right door.
